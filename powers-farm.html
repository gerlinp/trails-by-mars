Powers Farm Community Park

Trail Blazing Mom
August 15, 2025
592 N Main St, Randolph, MA 02368

“You’ve wandered alone in the woods to get away from it all, only to realize you’re standing in the middle of it all”
“You can never truly be alone in a forest.”
MGH 11/09/2024

Acquired in 2009 by the Town of Randolph, Massachusetts, through the Community Preservation Committee, Powers Farm Community Park is a 22-acre parcel that includes a diverse and dynamic ecosystem, nestled with meadows, marshland, and even a white cedar swamp. This park is located about 20 minutes south of Boston. It is easy to get to but can be overlooked, offering a sharp contrast to the busyness of Main Street.

The park is refreshing, with plenty of parking and public restrooms, making it perfect for families with young children. As you enter, you are greeted by a large pavilion providing shade on hot days or a place to rest before embarking on the short end-to-end walking adventure. The trail, which is packed dirt with some small rocks, is easy to stroll over, but it can get muddy and tough to navigate with a stroller if it rained earlier.

As you continue your stroll, you will encounter a wooden boardwalk over a swamp surrounded by cedar trees, creating almost total darkness as you make your way out of the shade back onto the trail, exiting to the pond. On a sunny day in spring, you will see ducks and swans congregating around, and depending on the season, the water is sometimes blanketed with green lily pads. Across the pond, there is a dock for kayaking, perfect for resting and taking pictures close to the water.
